About me

I am a physicist specialized in Experimental Particle Physics (M.Sc.) and History of Quantum Mechanics (Ph.D.). With a wide teaching experience, I have recently focus on the transference of high-quality scientific knowledge to a general audience—usually known as science vulgarisation.

Apart from my scholarly profile in research and teaching, I have tried many different ventures in my professional life. The most remarkable would be the co-foundation of a tuition center aimed at helping students with learning difficulties. However, I have also spent many hours of my life working hard at learning web design, statistical data analysis—what is nowadays famously called "big data"— or simply enjoying a good read or attempting a (not-so-good) writing.

If something does define me, this is my love of learning: I am always attracted to new challenges and refreshing personal and professional growth; and I'm not afraid to risk what I have secure to try and discover something new.

Well organized and systematic, to a certain level I enjoy orderly working environments with well-defined roles. But my Mediterranean roots don't betray me when I have to deal with some good improvisation.
